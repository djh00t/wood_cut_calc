About Timber Qualities

Timber quality grades help you categorize your inventory based on finish quality and intended use.

Benefits of specifying quality grades:

  • Match appropriate timber quality to project requirements
  • Organize inventory by structural vs. finish grade
  • Optimize costs by using appropriate quality for each purpose

Common examples include:

  • Premium - Highest quality, minimal defects, suitable for fine furniture
  • General Purpose - Good quality for most projects
  • Framing - Structural timber for building frames
  • Framing (Non-Structural) - Used for non-load bearing frames
